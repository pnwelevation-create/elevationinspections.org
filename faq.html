During the Inspection

A full home inspection covers all visible and accessible components of the property, including:

  • Roof, gutters, flashing, and drainage
  • Foundation, grading, and exterior walls
  • Electrical system — panel, wiring, outlets, GFCI
  • Plumbing — supply lines, drains, water heater, fixtures
  • HVAC — heating and cooling systems, ductwork
  • Attic — insulation, ventilation, visible framing
  • Basement and crawl space
  • Interior — ceilings, walls, floors, windows, doors
  • Built-in appliances
  • Fireplace and chimney (visible portions)
  • Garage — structure, door operation, safety features

A standard inspection is visual — it covers what's accessible and visible. It does not include:

  • Sewer lines (available as an add-on — bundle and save $100)
  • Inside walls, ceilings, or under flooring
  • Radon testing (ask about availability)
  • Mold testing (requires lab sampling)
  • Pest/termite inspection (requires a licensed pest inspector)
  • Swimming pools or hot tubs (available as add-on)
  • Detached structures unless specifically requested

Spokane-Specific Questions

Yes. Spokane County is in EPA Zone 1, which means it has the highest predicted average indoor radon levels in the country. Radon is a colorless, odorless gas that is the second leading cause of lung cancer in the US.

A standard home inspection does not include radon testing — it requires a separate test. Ask Dimitriy about radon testing availability when you schedule your inspection.

After 14 years and hundreds of inspections in the Spokane area, Dimitriy sees these issues most frequently:

  • Sewer line root intrusion — mature trees in older neighborhoods are the #1 culprit
  • Moisture in crawl spaces — Spokane's freeze-thaw cycles push water into foundations
  • Aging electrical panels — many homes still have Federal Pacific or Zinsco panels that are fire hazards
  • Chimney crown cracking — common after Spokane winters
  • Inadequate attic insulation — older homes weren't built to today's energy standards
  • Deferred roof maintenance — missing flashing, failed caulking around penetrations
