Radon Testing in Spokane, WA

Spokane sits in one of the highest radon zones in the Pacific Northwest. You can't see it, smell it, or taste it — but radon is the #2 cause of lung cancer in the U.S. Test before you close.

Why Spokane Is High Risk

Spokane Has Elevated Radon Levels

The EPA designates Spokane County as a Zone 1 radon area — the highest risk category — meaning average indoor radon levels are predicted to exceed 4 pCi/L, the EPA's action level. The geology of Eastern Washington, including granite-rich soils in many neighborhoods, naturally produces elevated radon gas.

Radon seeps up through foundation cracks, utility penetrations, and sump pits. It accumulates in lower levels of the home — basements, crawl spaces, and ground-floor living areas — where levels can be significantly higher than the national average.

1 in 6 Spokane homes tested exceed the EPA action level. A $150–200 test could save you from a $1,500+ mitigation bill — or worse — after closing.

How It Works

Simple, Non-Invasive Testing Process

1️⃣

Test Device Placed

A certified short-term radon test device is placed in the lowest livable area of the home — typically the basement or ground floor. The device requires 48–96 hours of closed-house conditions.

2️⃣

Closed-House Period

Windows and doors remain closed during the test period (normal entry and exit is fine). This simulates typical winter living conditions when radon accumulates most.

3️⃣

Device Retrieved & Analyzed

Dimitriy retrieves the device and sends it to a certified laboratory. Results are typically returned within 24–48 hours of lab receipt.

4️⃣

Results + Recommendations

You receive a written report with your radon level in pCi/L and clear guidance: whether the level is acceptable, borderline, or requires mitigation — and what mitigation typically costs.

Frequently Asked

Radon Testing Questions

How long does radon testing take?

Short-term tests require 48–96 hours on-site, plus 1–2 days for lab processing. You typically have results within 5–7 days of test placement. We can also do a 48-hour rush test for time-sensitive closings.

Can radon testing be bundled with a home inspection?

Yes — and it's recommended. Dimitriy can place the radon test device at the same time as the full home inspection. You receive both reports and can move forward with one appointment.

What happens if radon levels are high?

If results exceed 4 pCi/L, you have options: negotiate with the seller to install a mitigation system before closing, request a price reduction, or walk away. Radon mitigation in Spokane typically costs $800–$1,500 — a reasonable negotiating point on any transaction.

Do new homes need radon testing?

Yes. New construction is not immune — radon levels depend on soil and foundation design, not the age of the home. Many newer Spokane homes are built with passive radon systems; Dimitriy can evaluate whether the system is properly installed and functioning.
