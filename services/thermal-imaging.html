What Thermal Imaging Finds

Problems Hidden Inside Your Walls

Most home inspection defects are visible. These aren't — until you use infrared. Thermal imaging turns temperature differences into a visible image, revealing conditions that standard visual inspections miss entirely.

💧

Hidden Moisture & Leaks

Active leaks, roof intrusion, plumbing slow-leaks, and moisture buildup inside walls appear as cool spots on the thermal image — often before visible damage shows.

🧱

Insulation Gaps

Missing or improperly installed insulation shows up as thermal irregularities in exterior walls and ceilings — a significant energy and comfort issue in Spokane winters.

⚡

Electrical Hot Spots

Overloaded circuits, failing breakers, and loose connections generate heat. Thermal imaging catches these fire hazards before they become emergencies.

🌡️

HVAC & Duct Leaks

Air escaping from unsealed duct joints shows up in thermal. Significant duct leakage is common in older Spokane homes and drives up heating costs.

🪟

Window & Door Failures

Broken seals in double-pane windows and air infiltration around door frames appear clearly — issues that affect comfort and energy bills year-round.

🏗️

Foundation & Structural Moisture

Water intrusion at foundation walls and slab moisture often shows no visible staining yet — thermal catches the pattern early, before mold or structural damage sets in.

Common Questions

Thermal Imaging FAQ

Does thermal imaging see through walls?

Not exactly — but it reads surface temperature differences caused by what's behind the wall. A wet spot inside drywall cools the surface. A missing insulation section creates a thermal bridge. An overloaded wire heats the wall. Thermal imaging reads those signatures accurately.

Is thermal imaging worth it on a newer home?

Yes. New construction defects — insulation voids, missing vapor barriers, duct leaks — are actually easier to find with thermal on a new home because there's no history of repairs to obscure the readings. Electrical hot spots from installation errors are also common in new builds.

When is the best time for a thermal inspection in Spokane?

Fall through early spring — when there's at least a 15–18°F temperature differential between inside and outside. Spokane's winters make it ideal radon and thermal imaging season. Summer inspections are less optimal for envelope and insulation scans, but electrical and plumbing anomalies are visible year-round.

Do other Spokane inspectors use thermal imaging?

Some offer it as a paid add-on ranging from $75–$200 extra. At Elevation Inspections, Dimitriy includes thermal imaging in every full home inspection at no additional charge.
