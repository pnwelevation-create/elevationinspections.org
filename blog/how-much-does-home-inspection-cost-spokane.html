How Much Does a Home Inspection Cost in Spokane, WA?

Published by Dimitriy · Elevation Inspections · Updated 2025

One of the first questions Spokane homebuyers ask is: how much will a home inspection cost? The short answer: most full home inspections in Spokane run between $350 and $550, depending on the size and age of the property.

But the more useful question is: what do you get for that money — and where can you get the most value? Here's a complete breakdown from a licensed Spokane inspector.

Age matters too. Older homes — especially pre-1980 homes common in Spokane's South Hill, Browne's Addition, and North Side neighborhoods — tend to take longer to inspect due to older electrical systems, original plumbing, and more complex maintenance histories.

What's Included in a Standard Spokane Home Inspection

A full home inspection from a licensed inspector covers all major visible and accessible systems and components:

  • Roof covering, flashings, gutters, and downspouts
  • Foundation, structure, and grading
  • Electrical panel, wiring, and outlets
  • Plumbing supply lines, drains, and fixtures
  • Furnace, A/C, and ductwork
  • Attic insulation and ventilation
  • Interior walls, ceilings, floors, windows, and doors
  • Fireplace and chimney (visible components)
  • Garage and exterior

At Elevation Inspections, thermal imaging is included in every full inspection at no extra charge — Dimitriy uses a professional infrared camera to scan for hidden moisture, insulation gaps, and electrical hot spots that a standard visual inspection can miss.

What Affects Home Inspection Costs in Spokane

Property Size

Larger homes simply take more time. A 1,200 sq ft bungalow and a 3,800 sq ft home are two very different inspections. Most inspectors charge incrementally based on square footage.

Age of the Home

Older homes — particularly those built before 1960 — often have more complex systems to evaluate: knob-and-tube or aluminum wiring, galvanized plumbing, original oil furnaces, and foundations that have had decades to settle and crack.

Additional Structures

Detached garages, accessory dwelling units (ADUs), shops, or guest houses add time and typically cost extra.

Crawl Space vs. Basement

Crawl spaces take extra time to inspect properly and are often where the most important findings are. An inspector who skips a proper crawl inspection is leaving money on the table — for the seller.

Is a Home Inspection Worth It in Spokane?

Always. The median home price in Spokane is around $340,000–$400,000 depending on the neighborhood. A $400 inspection that catches a $12,000 sewer line repair, a $6,000 roof replacement, or a $4,000 electrical panel upgrade is one of the highest-ROI purchases you'll make in the home buying process.

More importantly: a good inspection report gives you leverage. If Dimitriy finds a furnace at end of life, a cracked heat exchanger, or roots in the sewer line — those are negotiating points that can reduce your purchase price or get repairs done before closing.
